Traditional Ukrainian borscht recipe

Ingredients

  • For the Broth:
  • 1½-2 liters of water
  • 400-500 grams of pork or beef on the bone
  • For the Sauté:
  • 2 small beets
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 3 medium onions
  • 4-5 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • A pinch of citric acid, a little table vinegar, or juice from ½ a lemon
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • For the Borscht:
  • 300 grams of fresh white cabbage
  • 4 medium potatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 dried bay leaves
  • Fresh herbs to taste
  • 1 clove of garlic (optional)
  • A pinch of ground cloves (optional)
  • A pinch of ground black pepper (optional)
  • Sour cream (optional, for serving)

Preparation

Cook the Broth

Pour cold water into a pot, add the meat, and place it over medium heat. The broth will be tastier if you use meat on the bone. Keep an eye on the broth and skim off the foam before boiling. When the liquid boils, cover the pan with a lid and simmer over low heat for an hour and a half.

Prepare the Sauté

Wash and peel the beets, carrot, and onions. Grate the beets on a coarse grater, the carrot on a medium grater, and dice the onions into small cubes. Pour the oil into a frying pan and heat it over medium heat. Sauté the onions and carrots, stirring, for about 5 minutes. Add the grated beets. Add citric acid, vinegar, or lemon juice. Cook the sauté for another 5 minutes, then add the tomato paste, stir, and let it cook for another 5-7 minutes.

Assemble the Borscht

Once the broth is ready, remove the meat from the pot. While it cools, add the shredded cabbage to the pot. After 5-10 minutes, add the diced or cubed potatoes. While the potatoes are cooking, separate the meat from the bones and cut it into cubes. Return the meat to the soup. Salt to taste. Add the sautéed vegetables and stir. Add the bay leaf and finely chopped herbs. Cover the pot with a lid and cook for another 5-7 minutes. Let the borscht sit under the lid for 5-10 minutes.

Serving the Borscht

For additional aroma, you can add some minced garlic, ground cloves, or black pepper. Borscht can be enjoyed immediately after cooking, but it usually tastes better the next day. Add sour cream and fresh herbs to each bowl. For extra tanginess, add a lemon wedge. Serve with rye bread or garlic buns. The dish also pairs well with lard and pampushki (Ukrainian garlic buns).
